###
Current AI safety auditing mostly examines individual agent traces for failures. | first bullet point
However, real-world violations often span multiple traces, revealing complex failure modes when analyzed collectively. | second bullet point
For example, ransomware attacks appear benign in single traces but reveal coordinated exfiltration patterns across many. | example sentence
Challenges include rarity, adversarial disguise, and missed detection by per-trace monitors. | challenges
Therefore, we need novel auditing approaches that identify violations across many traces simultaneously. | final bullet point

###
Previous work includes trace-level monitoring for sabotage and deception, but with limitations. | first bullet
Automated auditing through red teaming exists but often targets individual traces. | second bullet
Corpus-scale analysis helps discovery but relies on human-in-the-loop methods. | third bullet
Most methods miss complex hyperproperty failures across traces, a key limitation we address. | limitation bullet
Formal methods and runtime verification do not scale well nor handle natural-language properties. | final bullet
###
Our goal is to audit a repository of agent traces for safety violations specified by natural-language properties. | goal statement
Traditional methods classify traces independently, but OurMethod searches for violating sets or witnesses of traces. | first bullet
OurMethod involves embedding traces, clustering to reveal related groups, and building an agent prompt to combine these. | steps 1-3
We then use an agent reasoning system to propose witnesses, score traces, and emit verdicts: PASS, FAIL, or INCONCLUSIVE. | step 4 and outputs
This approach localizes and detects failures invisible to per-trace analysis. | conclusion sentence
